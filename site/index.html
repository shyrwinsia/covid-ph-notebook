This is a choropleth map that shows a population-normalized COVID-19 cases for municipalities and cities (Admin Level 3) of the Philippines.

For example, if City A has a population of 200,000 and 2 cases, it would be equal to City B which has a population of 50,000 with 1 case. Both having 1 cases per 100,000.

This map aims to make patterns visible on the spread of the disease and the effects of the community and government efforts. This only provides a "big picture" and does not show any interpretation.

All data comes from the public domain. Aggregation is done on best-effort and accuracy is not guaranteed. The author is not liable for any damages that result in the usage of this map.

Data is curated manually and this map may not be always up to date.
